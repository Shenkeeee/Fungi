Importance

Cultural

Fungi have been culturally significant for thousands of years, being used for food, medicine, spiritual purposes, and artistic inspiration. Today, they continue to be important as we discover more about their ecological and medicinal properties.

Role in the ecosystem

Fungi play a critical role in the ecosystem as decomposers, breaking down organic matter and recycling nutrients back into the soil. They also form symbiotic relationships with many plants, aiding in nutrient uptake and contributing to plant growth and overall ecosystem health.
